## Overview

The **Material Recovery Facility (MRF)** is a critical component of Putrajaya's sustainable waste management strategy, processing recyclable materials collected from across the city to reduce landfill dependency.

## Functions

### Waste Reception
- Incoming waste inspection
- Pre-sorting assessment
- Contamination removal
- Material categorization

### Sorting & Processing
- Automated sorting systems
- Manual quality control
- Material separation
- Baling and compaction

### Material Categories
- Paper and cardboard
- Plastics (various types)
- Metals (ferrous and non-ferrous)
- Glass
- E-waste components

## Process Flow

1. **Collection** - Recyclables collected from city
2. **Delivery** - Materials transported to MRF
3. **Inspection** - Quality and contamination check
4. **Sorting** - Separation by material type
5. **Processing** - Cleaning and preparation
6. **Baling** - Compression for transport
7. **Distribution** - Sale to recycling industries

## Environmental Impact

### Landfill Reduction
- Diverted materials from landfill
- Extended landfill lifespan
- Reduced land use

### Resource Conservation
- Material recovery for reuse
- Reduced virgin material extraction
- Energy savings in manufacturing

### Carbon Reduction
- Lower methane emissions
- Reduced manufacturing emissions
- Transportation efficiency

## Community Integration

### Education
- Facility tours for schools
- Recycling awareness programs
- Community outreach

### Economic Benefits
- Local employment
- Revenue from recovered materials
- Support for recycling industry
