## Overview

The **Putrajaya Lake Water Quality Monitoring System** is a critical component of the Integrated Environment Monitoring System (IEMS). With **5 strategically positioned monitoring stations** across Putrajaya Lake, this system provides real-time data on water quality, supporting lake management and environmental protection.

## Putrajaya Lake

### Importance

Putrajaya Lake is a defining feature of the city:

- **Size**: 650 hectares of man-made lake
- **Purpose**: Stormwater management, recreation, aesthetics
- **Ecosystem**: Home to diverse aquatic life
- **Recreation**: Water sports, tourism, leisure
- **Iconic**: Central to Putrajaya's identity

### Environmental Value

The lake provides:

- Natural cooling effect
- Biodiversity habitat
- Flood mitigation
- Water feature aesthetics
- Recreational opportunities

## Monitoring Network

### 5 Monitoring Stations

Stations are strategically located across the lake:

| Station | Location | Purpose |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Station 1 | Lake inlet area | Incoming water quality |
| Station 2 | Central lake | Main body monitoring |
| Station 3 | Marina area | High-activity zone |
| Station 4 | Wetland interface | Ecosystem health |
| Station 5 | Outlet area | Discharge quality |

### Coverage Strategy

The network design ensures:

- Representative sampling across lake
- Coverage of key ecological zones
- Monitoring of water flow patterns
- Detection of localized issues

## Parameters Monitored

### Physical Parameters

| Parameter | Description | Significance |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| Temperature | Water temperature | Ecosystem health |
| Turbidity | Water clarity | Light penetration |
| Conductivity | Dissolved solids | Salinity indicator |
| pH | Acidity/Alkalinity | Water chemistry |

### Chemical Parameters

| Parameter | Description | Significance |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| Dissolved Oxygen | O₂ in water | Aquatic life support |
| Ammonia | NH₃ levels | Pollution indicator |
| Nitrates | NO₃ levels | Nutrient loading |
| Phosphates | PO₄ levels | Eutrophication risk |

### Biological Indicators

- Chlorophyll-a (algae levels)
- Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD)
- Water color index

## Technology

### Sensor Technology

| Component | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Sensors | Multi-parameter water quality probes |
| Accuracy | High-precision calibrated sensors |
| Durability | Marine-grade, anti-fouling |
| Maintenance | Self-cleaning capability |

### Data Transmission

- **Frequency**: Real-time continuous monitoring
- **Communication**: Cellular/wireless transmission
- **Backup**: Local data storage
- **Security**: Encrypted data transfer

### IEMS Integration

The water quality data feeds into IEMS for:

- Centralized dashboard display
- Cross-correlation with other environmental data
- Unified alert system
- Comprehensive reporting

| Condition | Response |
