## Overview

The **Micro Mobility Service by Tryke** is an electric scooter sharing program that provides sustainable, convenient, and affordable last-mile transportation options for Putrajaya's residents, workers, and visitors. This initiative supports the city's environmental goals while addressing the critical first/last-mile connectivity challenge.

## Service Features

### Electric Scooters

The fleet consists of modern, well-maintained electric scooters:

| Specification | Details |
|---------------|---------|
| Type | Standing electric scooters |
| Range | Up to 25 km per charge |
| Max Speed | 25 km/h (safety limited) |
| Weight Capacity | Up to 100 kg |
| Safety Features | Front/rear lights, bell, reflectors |

### Mobile App

The user-friendly Tryke app provides:

- **Account Registration**: Quick sign-up process
- **Scooter Location**: Real-time map of available scooters
- **QR Code Unlock**: Scan to start your ride
- **Ride Tracking**: GPS-based route and duration
- **Payment**: Cashless in-app transactions
- **Ride History**: Past trips and receipts

## How It Works

### Getting Started

1. **Download App**: Available on iOS and Android
2. **Register**: Create account with valid ID
3. **Add Payment**: Link credit/debit card or e-wallet
4. **Safety Tutorial**: Complete in-app safety briefing

### Taking a Ride

1. **Find Scooter**: Locate nearby scooter on app map
2. **Scan & Unlock**: Scan QR code to unlock
3. **Ride**: Follow traffic rules and designated paths
4. **Park Properly**: End ride at designated parking zone
5. **Pay**: Automatic fare deduction

### Pricing Structure

| Component | Rate |
|-----------|------|
| Unlock Fee | RM 1.00 |
| Per Minute | RM 0.30 |
| Pause Rate | RM 0.10/min |
| Daily Pass | RM 15.00 |

*Prices subject to promotional adjustments*

## Coverage Areas

### Service Zones

The micro mobility service operates in key areas:

- **Precinct 1-5**: Government and commercial areas
- **Precinct 8-9**: Mixed-use developments
- **Putrajaya Sentral**: Transport hub connectivity
- **Recreational Areas**: Parks and lakeside zones

### Designated Parking

Dedicated parking zones ensure orderly operations:
- Clearly marked parking bays
- Near public transport stops
- At building entrances
- In recreational areas

## Safety Guidelines

### Rider Requirements
- Minimum age: 18 years
- Valid identification
- Completed safety tutorial
- Helmet recommended (available at select locations)

### Riding Rules
- Ride on designated paths and bike lanes
- Maximum one rider per scooter
- No riding under influence of alcohol
- Obey all traffic signals and signs
- Yield to pedestrians

### Prohibited Areas
- Pedestrian-only zones
- Indoor areas
- Highways and main roads
- Restricted government compounds

## Integration with Public Transport

The service is designed to complement existing public transportation:

### First/Last Mile Solution
- Connect to Putrajaya Sentral (ERL station)
- Link to bus stops and routes
- Access to monorail stations (future)

### Multi-Modal Journey
1. Take public transport to nearest station
2. Use micro mobility for final stretch
3. Park at designated zone near destination
