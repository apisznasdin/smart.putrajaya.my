## Overview

The **Water Level Monitoring Stations** network is a critical flood prevention and water management infrastructure. With **11 stations across Putrajaya Wetland** and **1 station at Putrajaya Dam**, this system provides real-time water level data for flood early warning and water resource management.

## Network Configuration

### Station Distribution

| Location | Stations | Purpose |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Putrajaya Wetland | 11 stations | Wetland water management |
| Putrajaya Dam | 1 station | Dam level monitoring |
| **Total** | **12 stations** | Comprehensive coverage |

### Wetland Stations (11)

Strategically positioned across the wetland system:

- **Inlet Points**: Monitor incoming water
- **Wetland Cells**: Track water within system
- **Outlet Points**: Monitor discharge
- **Critical Zones**: High-risk areas

### Dam Station (1)

Dedicated monitoring at Putrajaya Dam:

- **Dam Level**: Water storage level
- **Spillway Status**: Overflow monitoring
- **Trend Analysis**: Storage patterns

## Flood Early Warning

### Alert Levels

| Level | Status | Water Level | Action |
|-------|--------|-------------|--------|
| Green | Normal | Within normal range | Routine monitoring |
| Yellow | Alert | Approaching threshold | Increased monitoring |
| Orange | Warning | High level | Prepare response |
| Red | Critical | Danger level | Emergency response |

### Warning Chain

1. **Detection**: Sensor detects rising water
2. **Analysis**: System correlates with rainfall
3. **Alert**: Automated notification triggered
4. **Verification**: Operations team confirms
5. **Response**: Emergency protocols activated
6. **Communication**: Public notification if needed
